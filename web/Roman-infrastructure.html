The Roman Empire was famous for its incredible infrastructure. The Romans built roads, aqueducts, bridges, and buildings that still stand today. Their engineering skills were unmatched in the ancient world.

Roman roads connected the entire empire and allowed for fast communication and trade. These roads were so well-built that many are still used today, over 2,000 years later.

Aqueducts were engineering marvels that brought fresh water to cities from distant sources. The Romans built hundreds of aqueducts throughout their empire to supply water to millions of people.

Types of Roman Infrastructure

Type Description Example
Roads Connected cities and allowed trade and communication Via Appia
Aqueducts Brought fresh water to cities Pont du Gard
Bridges Crossed rivers and valleys Pons Fabricius
Buildings Temples, theaters, and public baths Colosseum
